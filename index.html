Welcome to The Thinkery!

Your gateway to better thinking.

treasury of interactive thinking activities for the classroom. Each one asks you to sort, classify, rank or connect ideas — and then defend your choices. The interesting learning lives in the grey areas. Choose a chapter below and open an activity to begin. Explore the subjects ▸

I Sociology Deviance & Social Norms

Deviant or Illegal?

Sort behaviours into "deviant but not illegal" and "illegal but not deviant" — and wrestle with the cases that are both, or neither.

Thinking skill: classifying & distinguishing Open ▸

Folkways or Mores?

Decide which everyday expectations are minor customs and which carry real moral weight — and notice how the line shifts across cultures.

Thinking skill: judging moral weight Open ▸

How Serious Is It?

Rank nine deviant acts from most to least serious in a forced diamond — then share your arrangement to the class screen and defend your order.

Thinking skill: prioritising & justifying Open ▸

Community to Society

Place relationships and institutions along the Gemeinschaft–Gesellschaft line, then mark where modern society sits — and share it to the class screen.

Thinking skill: judging degree & transition Open ▸
II English Rhetoric & Persuasion

Ethos, Pathos, Logos

Classify persuasive examples by their primary appeal — credibility, emotion, or logic — then unpack the ones that blend all three.
